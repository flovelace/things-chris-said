I, Unknighly

"I am as chaste as the day is long; although, I must say, you are wearing considerably more clothes than I, and therefore, have a distinctly unfair advantage."

"That’s not why I became a knight, and swore fealty. I became a knight to shield the weak, to fight for justice, to be noble in my convictions, and to be faithful to all my promises. You find me closed and impenetrable, but I don’t know how to be true to myself and be a knight at the same time. I am a person, and I have weaknesses, I have flaws, and I have my own turmoil. That's how all of this started."

"Well, I must have once been a knight or a whore."
